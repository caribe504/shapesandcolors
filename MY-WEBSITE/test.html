Starting with this arangement of platonic solids, we can observe the relationships between the shapes and colors as the tetrahedron cube and octahedron, the smallest, appear with a green-blue color reflecting their pureness, the dodecahedron and icosahedron, with 12 faces and 21 vertices and 21 faces and 12 vertices respectively are represented by hotter, more rounder colors.

Following, we have this collection of prisms and pyramids. One of the key characteristics of these is that they are connected, if a prism has a color, the pyramid with the corresponding base will absorb that same color no matter how far away they are from eachother. Art entanglement.
